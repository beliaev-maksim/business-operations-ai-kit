## 2. Task Breakdown

[The following tasks are broken down by phase. This structure ensures that foundational work is completed before execution, and that the project is properly closed out.]

### Phase 1: Planning & Discovery (Weeks 1-2)

**Goal**: To finalize the project scope, align stakeholders, and prepare for execution.

| Task ID | Description | Owner | Effort (Days) | Definition of Done (Acceptance Criteria) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| T-01 | **Schedule & Conduct Kickoff Meeting**: Align all core team members and key stakeholders on the project goals, scope, and timeline. | [Project Manager] | 1 | Meeting held; minutes and action items distributed. |
| T-02 | **Finalize Stakeholder RACI**: Confirm the roles and responsibilities of all stakeholders. | [Project Manager] | 0.5 | RACI chart approved by the executive sponsor. |
| T-03 | **Detailed Requirements Gathering**: Conduct workshops with SMEs and affected teams to document detailed functional and non-functional requirements. | [Business Analyst] | 2 | Requirements document signed off by the problem owner. |
| T-04 | **Develop Detailed Project Plan & Timeline**: Create a comprehensive timeline with milestones, dependencies, and critical path. | [Project Manager] | 1 | Project plan approved by the executive sponsor. |

### Phase 2: Design & Prototyping (Weeks 3-4)

**Goal**: To design the solution and validate it with users before full implementation.

| Task ID | Description | Owner | Effort (Days) | Definition of Done (Acceptance Criteria) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| T-05 | **Create Process Flow Diagrams (To-Be)**: Map out the new workflow that the solution will enable. | [Business Analyst] | 2 | Process flows validated with the process owner and SMEs. |
| T-06 | **Develop Wireframes/Mockups (if applicable)**: Create visual representations of any new interfaces or tools. | [UX/UI Designer] | 3 | Mockups approved by the problem owner and a sample of end-users. |
| T-07 | **Build a Proof of Concept (PoC)**: Develop a small-scale, functional version of the solution to test feasibility. | [Lead Developer] | 5 | PoC successfully demonstrates the core functionality and addresses the main technical risks. |
| T-08 | **User Acceptance Testing (UAT) on PoC**: Have a group of end-users test the PoC and provide feedback. | [QA Lead] | 2 | UAT feedback collected, and a "go/no-go" decision is made on the design. |

### Phase 3: Implementation & Testing (Weeks 5-8)

**Goal**: To build, test, and prepare the full solution for rollout.

| Task ID | Description | Owner | Effort (Days) | Definition of Done (Acceptance Criteria) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| T-09 | **Full Solution Development**: Build the solution based on the approved design and PoC feedback. | [Development Team] | 10 | All features are code-complete and have passed unit tests. |
| T-10 | **Integration Testing**: Test the solution's interaction with other systems. | [QA Lead] | 3 | All integration points are working as expected, and data flows correctly. |
| T-11 | **End-to-End (E2E) Testing**: Conduct a full test of the user journey. | [QA Lead] | 4 | The E2E test plan is fully executed with a pass rate of 95% or higher. |
| T-12 | **Develop Training Materials & Documentation**: Create user guides, FAQs, and training videos. | [Training Lead] | 5 | All materials are reviewed and approved by the problem owner. |

### Phase 4: Rollout & Change Management (Weeks 9-10)

**Goal**: To deploy the solution and ensure users are prepared and supported.

| Task ID | Description | Owner | Effort (Days) | Definition of Done (Acceptance Criteria) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| T-13 | **Conduct User Training Sessions**: Train all affected users on the new process and/or tool. | [Training Lead] | 3 | At least 90% of the target audience has completed training. |
| T-14 | **Execute Communication Plan**: Send out all planned communications about the launch. | [Comms Lead] | 1 | All communications sent on schedule. |
| T-15 | **Deploy Solution to Production**: Go-live with the new solution. | [DevOps/IT] | 1 | Solution is live and accessible to all users. |
| T-16 | **Provide Go-Live Support (Hypercare)**: Have a dedicated team ready to address any immediate issues or questions from users. | [Support Lead] | 5 | All critical (P1) issues are resolved within 4 hours; all other issues are triaged within 24 hours. |

### Phase 5: Measurement & Closure (Weeks 11-12)

**Goal**: To measure the impact of the solution and formally close the project.

| Task ID | Description | Owner | Effort (Days) | Definition of Done (Acceptance Criteria) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| T-17 | **Monitor & Measure KPIs**: Track the problem-oriented KPIs for at least 30 days post-launch. | [Business Analyst] | 2 | A report is generated showing the change in KPIs from baseline to post-launch. |
| T-18 | **Conduct Post-Implementation Review**: Hold a "lessons learned" session with the project team and key stakeholders. | [Project Manager] | 1 | Lessons learned are documented and shared. |
| T-19 | **Handover to Business Owner**: Formally transition ownership of the new process/tool to the business-as-usual (BAU) owner. | [Project Manager] | 0.5 | Handover document is signed off by the BAU owner. |
| T-20 | **Final Project Report & Closure**: Prepare a final report for the executive sponsor and archive all project artifacts. | [Project Manager] | 1 | Project is formally closed. |
